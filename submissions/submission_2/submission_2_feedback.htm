Note: choosing number of clusters
It can often be ambiguous determining whether silhouette score, or any other measure of clustering performance, gives a definitive answer to the "correct" number of clusters in a dataset.

For example, Silhouette score is based on Euclidean distance, which is the metric used to form clusters in K-Means — this could produce scores that are well-suited to K-Means but not necessarily to GMM.

While using GMM you can also look at the Bayesian Information Criterion (BIC) or the Akaike information criterion (AIC) for finding the optimal number of clusters.
